Hi! I am Ngoc Luyen Le, currently working as a Research and Development Engineer specializing in Natural Language Processing (NLP), Large Language Models (LLMs), Semantic Representation, and Recommender Systems.
Previously, I held a postdoctoral research position at the Heudiasyc Laboratory of the Université de Technologie de Compiègne, under the supervision of Professor Marie-Hélène Abel. My work focused on recommender systems and knowledge representation, particularly in vehicle-related applications. Prior to that, I earned my PhD from the LAB-STICC Laboratory at IMT Atlantique, France, under the supervision of Professors Yannis Haralambous and Philippe Lenca. My doctoral research explored semantic representation for the French language using Combinatory Categorial Grammar (CCG) and Discourse Representation Theory (DRT).
My current research focuses on applying advanced NLP techniques, LLMs, and Generative AI to enhance semantic modeling and optimize recommender systems across various application domains. I am particularly interested in developing context-aware, linguistically informed systems capable of understanding and responding to human language in specialized areas such as skill management and education. Through this work, I aim to contribute to the development of intelligent, adaptive technologies for real-world applications.
